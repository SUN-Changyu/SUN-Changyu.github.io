Hi, I am Changyu Sun

Neuroscientist, bioinformatician, science-lover

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Neurobiology at University of Chicago.

I am amazed by how neural circuits with just a few interconnected neurons manage to perform sophisticated functions in learning and memory.

My research primarily focuses on how inhibitory circuits mediate olfactory learning and memory.

Projects

Learning & Memory

"Cells that fire together wire together." Hebbian theory suggests that assemblies are formed during sensory learning by strengthening the excitatory connections between co-active neurons, which is the basis of associative learning and memory.
I am using olfactory cortex as a model to study changes in synaptic strength and neural activity with odor learning.

  • Hebbian plasticity
  • Cortical assembly
  • Olfactory learning

Circuits & Synpases

VIP neurons preferentially inhibit other interneurons thus promotes LTP in pyramidal neurons via disinhibitory circuit.
I am studying the activation mechanism of VIP neurons that gate the synaptic plasticity of exciatatory neurons and give rise to assembly formation in olfactory cortex.

  • Inhibitory circuit
  • Synaptic plasticity
  • VIP neurons

Modeling

Building a network model with excitatory neurons and different classes of interneurons will allow us to have better understanding on the circuit mechanisms of selective assembly formation and/or suppression.

  • Network model
